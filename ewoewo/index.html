A Letter to My Chinu

March 28, 2022 — March 28, 2026

To the one who ruined all other people for me,

It is midnight. We have officially been together for four years. FOUR. Do you know what that means? That means I have chosen you — specifically you, with all your chaos and all your beauty — every single day for 1,461 days. That is not a small thing. That is actually insane. I am insane. About you.

✦ ✦ ✦

I keep trying to figure out exactly when it happened — the moment you went from being just a person in my life to being THE person in my life. And I genuinely cannot pinpoint it. It wasn't one big dramatic moment. It was quieter than that. It was laughing so hard at something stupid that my stomach hurt. It was the way you exist so effortlessly. It was realizing, at some completely random moment, that I didn't want to be anywhere you weren't. And that scared me. And thrilled me. Mostly thrilled me. Four years ago I did not fully understand what I was signing up for. I did not know that I was agreeing to have someone who would eat the last of something I was saving and feel zero guilt about it. I did not know I was agreeing to have someone who gives the WORST movie suggestions sometimes and then gets personally offended when I say it was terrible. I did not know I was signing up for someone who takes up emotional and physical space in every single room they walk into. And I would sign up for all of it again. Instantly. Without even pausing to think. Can I be honest? Like embarrassingly, vulnerably honest? Good. Because it's midnight and sentimentality hits different at midnight. You make me want to be a better version of myself, not because you pressure me to be — you literally never do — but because seeing YOU be so effortlessly yourself, so fully and unapologetically YOU, makes me want to match that energy. You walk into spaces like you belong there because you genuinely believe you do. And slowly, quietly, you've made me believe I do too. I don't think you know how much that matters. I don't think you know how many times your confidence — not arrogance, but that quiet, settled confidence — has pulled me out of my own head when I was spiraling. You didn't even know you were doing it. You were probably just existing normally. I was having a whole internal transformation. Classic us, honestly. I love the tiny things. I know everyone says that in love letters but I mean it in such a specific way. I love the way you tell stories — how you always start three steps too early and include details that have absolutely nothing to do with the point but somehow make the story SO much better. I love how you get weirdly passionate about things and then drag me into caring about them too and suddenly I have opinions about things I did not care about six months ago. I love how you laugh — properly laugh, from your whole body — when something catches you off guard. I love that you are somehow both the most practical person and the most romantic person I know and you don't even see those two things as contradictions. I love how you see the world. It's different from how I see it, and better in the places where it counts. Okay here's the part of the letter where I get a little embarrassing and I need you to bear with me. You know how some people just… fit? Like not perfectly, not without friction, not like two puzzle pieces in a cheesy metaphor — but like two people who have decided, consciously and repeatedly, that they want to navigate the world together? That's us. We are not a perfect love story. We are a real one. And I think real is so much better. We have had hard days. Days where the weight of everything felt too much and we didn't handle it perfectly and we were just two very tired, very human people doing our best. And still — still — you chose to stay and figure it out. And so did I. And that means more to me than any grand gesture ever could. Love that survives the boring parts and the hard parts and the weird parts — that's the love I want. That's the love I have with you. And we have had SUCH good days. Days that I would put in a jar and keep forever if I could. Ordinary days that somehow felt like the best days of my life just because you were in them. Late nights talking about everything and nothing. Inside jokes that I can't explain to anyone else without killing the whole thing. The way some songs now permanently belong to us. The specific way you say my name sometimes and I feel like the luckiest person alive for absolutely no logical reason. We have built an entire world, you and I. A language. A whole vocabulary of memories. I am so proud of that. Of us.

✦ ✦ ✦

Four years down. Every single one after this too, please.

Forever yours , A.Chitransh 💗
